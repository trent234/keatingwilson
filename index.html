01 / The practice

A small practice for large insurance programmes.

Independent senior advisory on life and annuity platform programmes — conversions, consolidations, reinsurance block migrations, and the product-configuration work that sits underneath all of them.

Most engagements are arranged through Tier-1 insurance software providers — the firms that run policy administration platforms for major carriers and reinsurers. Some engagements are arranged with carriers or reinsurers directly.

Engagements are typically multi-year, sole-practitioner arrangements at the senior advisory level. The practice does not staff teams, run RFPs, or compete on scale of consulting headcount.

Client segments
Where the work sits in the industry.
The practice works with the following types of organisation, either directly or through their administration platform vendor.
  • Life and annuity carriers
  • Closed-book consolidators
  • Life and annuity reinsurers, including Bermuda
  • Pan-regional life assurance groups
  • Tier-1 administration platform providers
02 / Engagement model

How the practice is set up.

Four facts that describe what working with the practice actually looks like.

i.
Single principal.
Steven Wilson is the engagement. The practice does not staff teams, run delivery operations, or compete on consulting headcount. All client-facing work is performed personally.
ii.
Multi-year retainers.
Engagements are typically structured as retainers spanning specific programme phases — usually measured in quarters and years rather than weeks.
iii.
Direct or alongside the platform vendor.
Engagements run either with the carrier or reinsurer directly, or alongside their Tier-1 platform vendor's delivery team. Most current work is the latter.
iv.
Geographic scope.
Current and recent engagements span the United States, the United Kingdom, continental Europe, Bermuda, and Sub-Saharan Africa.

05 / Principal
SKW
Steven K. Wilson · Founder & Principal

Steven K. Wilson.

Steven founded VERTEX, Incorporated in New Britain, Connecticut in 1991. Over the next twenty-three years, the firm grew to 190 employees, with the majority of its revenue from life and annuity insurers and nearly half from tier-one carriers.

VERTEX's VTXml Product Suite was the most widely used tool in the industry for the definition and publication of insurance product information using ACORD's PPfA, PPfL, and related data models. The product was recognised by ACORD for ten consecutive years and received the 2012 RAVE Award.

In November 2014, VERTEX was acquired by Ebix, Inc. (NASDAQ: EBIX). Steven completed the integration as Senior Vice President of Ebix Consulting and departed in 2015. He returned to active client work in 2024 through Keating Wilson Co.
